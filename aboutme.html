About Me

My Radio Background

I first heard of ham radio around 2013 from my uncle Ron who mentioned he had heard a voice coming through on his car FM radio and thought it might be interference from a ham radio. I didn't know what ham radio was at the time but his story interested me and later I looked into finding out exactly what IS a ham radio?? What I read made me curious for more. After a coworker mentioned our company had a radio club, I became a member of the W6TRW club and got my technician license shortly after.

I'm interested in building antennas, SOTA, satellites, and more recently the microwave bands.

My radio equipment includes an Icom 705 w/various antennas, Yaesu FT-60 & Anytone 878 handhelds, Pluto SDR, DEMI 10GHz transverter & slot antenna.

I like to use my club W6TRW shack for most of my HF operating. It has remote capabilities and I can operate from home or anywhere with internet connection. Located in Redondo Beach, the W6TRW shack is an amazing setup with 2 antenna towers with rotatable masts hosting 40M, 6M, 2M beams, a UHF/VHF vertical, & 80M inverted V on one tower and 20M, 17M, 15M, 10M, 6M on the second tower. The main radio is a Flex 6600 with a Maestro headunit that interface with the Flex Antenna Genius for antenna switching. The club amplifiers are a Elecraft KPA500 and Henry 1.5kW amp.

Hope to hear you on the air!

73,

Dylon N6MX
